Selected Articles & Publications

Peer-reviewed research on biliteracy policy, language teaching, and multilingual education. Click any title to read a summary.

2025

ChatGPT in Foreign Language Lesson Plan Creation: Trends, Variability, and Historical Biases

Dyrnburg, A. & Davin, K. J. (2025). ReCALL, 37(3), 332–347.

doi.org/10.1017/S0958344024000272

Examines how ChatGPT generates foreign language lesson plans, identifying patterns in instructional approach, variability across prompts, and evidence of historical biases in the AI's output related to language teaching methodology.
2024

Stories of Struggle and Resilience: Examining the Experiences of Two Spanish Teachers Through History in Person

Davin, K. J., & Donato, R. (2024). Modern Language Journal, 108(3), 670–687.

doi.org/10.1111/modl.12947

Uses a history-in-person framework to trace the professional trajectories of two Spanish teachers, revealing how personal struggles and resilience shape their classroom practices and professional identities over time.
2023

Preparing Cherokee Teachers in a World Language Education Program

Kissau, S., Davin, K., & Moore, K. (2023). Journal of Teacher Education.

doi.org/10.1177/00224871231165478

Explores a unique teacher preparation program designed to train Cherokee language teachers within a world language education framework, examining the challenges and opportunities of preserving Indigenous languages through formal education pathways.
2022

A Sociocultural Perspective: Seeking Evidence of Development in a PACE Approach to Grammar Instruction

Davin, K. J., & Kushki, A. (2022). Language Learning, 72(S1), 165–200.

doi.org/10.1111/lang.12473

Applies sociocultural theory to examine how the PACE model of grammar instruction supports second language development, providing evidence of student growth through mediated interaction in the foreign language classroom.
2021

Contradictions in Teachers' Classroom Dynamic Assessment Implementation: An Activity System Analysis

Sagan, A., Heinion, J. B., & Davin, K. J. (2021). TESOL Quarterly.

doi.org/10.1002/tesq.3046

Uses activity theory to analyze the contradictions that arise when teachers attempt to implement dynamic assessment in their classrooms, identifying systemic tensions between institutional demands and assessment-for-learning approaches.
2020

Implementing the Seal of Biliteracy: A Multiple Case Study of Six High-Awarding Districts

Heineke, A. J., & Davin, K. J. (2020). Modern Language Journal, 104(2), 395–411.

doi.org/10.1111/modl.12708

Examines six school districts that award high numbers of Seals of Biliteracy to identify common practices, policies, and conditions that support successful biliteracy program implementation at scale.
2019

Critical Language Testing: Factors Influencing Students' Decisions to (Not) Pursue the Seal of Biliteracy

Davin, K. J. (2019). Harvard Educational Review, 91(2), 179–203.

doi.org/10.17763/1943-5045-91.2.179

Investigates the factors that influence whether students choose to pursue the Seal of Biliteracy, applying a critical language testing lens to understand how testing policies and perceptions shape student participation in biliteracy recognition.
2019

Recruitment and Retention: Analyzing Foreign Language Teachers' Motivation, Influences and Perceptions

Kissau, S., Davin, K. J., & Wang, C. (2019). Teaching and Teacher Education, 76, 174–183.

doi.org/10.1016/j.tate.2018.11.020

Analyzes the motivations, influences, and perceptions of foreign language teachers to better understand patterns in recruitment and retention, offering insights into addressing the ongoing shortage of world language educators.
2018

The Genesis of Classroom Discursive Practices as History in Person Processes

Donato, R., & Davin, K. J. (2018). Language Teaching Research, 23(6), 739–760.

doi.org/10.1177/1362168817925473

Traces how classroom discursive practices emerge as expressions of teachers' personal and professional histories, showing how past experiences shape the communicative patterns teachers develop in their language classrooms.
2018

The Seal of Biliteracy: Successes and Challenges of Implementation

Davin, K. J., Heineke, A. J., & Egnatz, L. (2018). Foreign Language Annals, 51(2), 275–289.

doi.org/10.1111/flan.12336

Presents findings on both the successes and the challenges schools face when implementing the Seal of Biliteracy, identifying key factors that support or hinder effective program rollout across different educational contexts.
2018

The Seal of Biliteracy: Adding Students' Voices to the Conversation

Davin, K. J., & Heineke, A. J. (2018). Bilingual Research Journal, 41(3), 312–328.

doi.org/10.1080/15235882.2018.1481896

Centers the perspectives of students who have pursued the Seal of Biliteracy, examining their experiences, motivations, and reflections on what biliteracy recognition means to their identity and future aspirations.
2017

The Seal of Biliteracy: Variations in Policy and Outcomes

Davin, K. J., & Heineke, A. J. (2017). Foreign Language Annals, 50(3), 486–499.

doi.org/10.1111/flan.12279

Compares Seal of Biliteracy policies across multiple states, documenting significant variations in requirements, assessment criteria, and award outcomes, and discussing implications for equity and program quality.
